Undergraduate Programs

The Department offers two undergraduate degree programmes, Bachelor of Science in Computer Science Single-Major (3:2:1:1) and Major-Minor (3:2:2:1) as well as Bachelor of Science in Information Technology.

Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSc IT)

The BSc IT program is designed to provide students with a solid foundation in information technology, covering areas such as software development, database management, and network administration.

Bachelor of Computer Science (BCS)

The BCS program offers students the flexibility to choose a Single-major or Major-minor from a variety of computer science programs:

  • Single-Major:

    • To qualify for admission into Computer Science Single-major programmes at Level 200, candidates must have taken the following courses at Level 100: MATH 121: Algebra and Trigonometry MATH 122: Calculus I CSCD 101: Introduction to Computer Science I CSCD 102: Introduction to Computer Science II

Graduate Programs
Master's Programs

  • Master of Science in Computer Science (MSc CS): An advanced program covering research and practical aspects of computer science.

  • Master of Science in Information Technology (MSc IT): Focuses on advanced IT concepts, strategic planning, and management.

Research Programs

  • Master of Philosophy in Computer Science (MPhil CS): A research-focused program with a thesis requirement.

  • Doctor of Philosophy in Computer Science (PhD CS): A comprehensive doctoral program for advanced research in computer science.
